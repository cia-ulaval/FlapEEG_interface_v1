Consent Form for EEG Recording Protocol
Study Title: EEG Recording for Blinking Event Detection and Model Training
Principal Investigator: Louis-Etienne Messier
Institution: CIA
Contact Information: louis-etienne.messier.1@ulaval.ca
Introduction:
You are being invited to participate in a study aimed at recording and annotating electroencephalogram (EEG) signals related to blinking events. The data collected will be used for training, analysis, and building models to better understand blink patterns. Please read the following information carefully before agreeing to participate.
Objective:
The purpose of this study is to collect EEG signals associated with blinking events in order to train and analyze models that can detect and classify blink-related data for research purposes.
Equipment:
EEG device with at least 8 electrodes, including frontal electrodes (e.g., Fp1, Fp2) for optimal blink detection.
Computer with EEG recording software and storage capacity.
Comfortable chair and quiet room setup.
Video recording device for synchronized blink validation.
Data Collection Procedure:
EEG recording will begin, and time-stamped annotations will be made for each task and corresponding stimuli.
The tasks will be performed in the specified order.
Data will be annotated in real time (e.g., voluntary, involuntary, or stimulated blink).
You will be monitored to ensure your comfort and that you follow the instructions provided.
Potential Risks:
There are no known significant risks associated with EEG recording. However, some discomfort may occur from the placement of the electrodes or from sitting in one position for an extended period.
If you feel uncomfortable or wish to stop the experiment at any time, you may do so without penalty.
Confidentiality:
All data collected during this study will be kept confidential and used solely for research purposes.
Your personal information will not be shared, and the data will be anonymized for analysis and model training.
Voluntary Participation:
Your participation is completely voluntary, and you may withdraw from the study at any time without any consequences.
By signing this form, you acknowledge that you have been informed about the study, its procedures, and any risks, and that you voluntarily agree to participate.
